In this thesis, we explored methods to simultaneously measure electrodermal activity (EDA) and electromyography (EMG) from the same muscle. The aim was to determine whether these biomarkers could be used to further detect stress in nonverbal patients. This thesis utilized galvanic isolation between the EDA and EMG circuits. EDA was an active measurement, and EMG was a passive measurement. By galvanically isolating these two circuits, we achieved a filtration method that separated the two measurement methods, preventing interference between them. Galvanic isolation seemed like the way to separate these two circuits and to avoid ground loops. The simultaneous measurement results were reasonable compared to EDA or EMG measurements done separately. With these findings, the opportunity to use the galvanic isolation method to separate passive and active measurements was limitless. Getting more information about the body simultaneously created a huge opportunity for medical diagnostics to see the bigger picture.
